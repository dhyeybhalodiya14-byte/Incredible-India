TASTE INDIA

Every State Has Its Own Flavor

Indian cuisine is a kaleidoscope of flavors, aromas and textures shaped by geography, religion, and culture. From the buttery richness of North Indian curries to the coconut-laced dishes of the South, from the fiery street food of Mumbai to the sweet Bengali mishti — India's food is as diverse as its people.

BY REGION

Regional Cuisines

Gujarat
Thepla

Thepla in a rich, creamy tomato-based sauce — one of India's most iconic dishes worldwide.

Awadh (UP)
Lucknowi Biryani

Aromatic dum-cooked rice with tender meat, saffron and whole spices — the Nawabi legacy lives on.

Punjab
Dal Makhani

Slow-cooked black lentils with butter and cream — a hearty Punjabi staple loved all over India.

Delhi / Punjab
Chole Bhature

Spicy chickpea curry with fluffy deep-fried bread — the ultimate North Indian breakfast.

Tamil Nadu / Karnataka
Masala Dosa

Crispy fermented crepe filled with spiced potatoes, served with sambar and chutneys.

Hyderabad
Hyderabadi Biryani

The crown jewel of South Indian cuisine — fragrant rice layered with spiced meat cooked in the dum style.

Kerala
Kerala Fish Curry

Fish cooked with coconut milk, kokum and spices — tangy, spicy and utterly delicious.

Tamil Nadu
Idli Sambar

Steamed rice cakes with lentil vegetable stew — the ultimate healthy South Indian comfort food.

Mumbai
Vada Pav

Mumbai's beloved street food — spiced potato fritter in a bun with chutneys. The city's soul food.

Gujarat
Dhokla

Steamed fermented chickpea flour snack — soft, spongy, tangy and light. A Gujarati classic.

Goa
Goan Fish Curry

A tangy coconut-based fish curry with Goan spices — the perfect seaside meal.

Maharashtra / MP
Kanda Poha

Flattened rice cooked with onions, turmeric, peanuts and herbs — a classic morning comfort dish.

West Bengal
Rasgulla

Soft cottage cheese balls soaked in light sugar syrup — Bengal's most celebrated sweet dessert.

Sikkim / Northeast
Momos

Steamed dumplings stuffed with spiced meat or vegetables — a Northeast Indian street food staple.

West Bengal / Odisha
Machher Jhol

A delicate Bengali fish curry with mustard and turmeric — simple, fragrant and soulful.

Bihar / Jharkhand
Litti Chokha

Wheat balls stuffed with sattu, roasted and served with mashed vegetables — rustic and earthy.

THE SECRET INGREDIENT

The Spice Trail of India

India is the world's largest producer, consumer and exporter of spices. The ancient trade routes were built on the allure of Indian spices — turmeric, cardamom, pepper, saffron, cumin and hundreds more.
