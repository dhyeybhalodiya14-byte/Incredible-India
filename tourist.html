Tourist Places

State by State · City by City · Wonder by Wonder

Rajasthan

The Land of Kings — forts, palaces, deserts and royal heritage

Jaipur
Hawa Mahal

The iconic "Palace of Winds" with 953 small windows, built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh.

Jaipur, Rajasthan
Udaipur
City Palace, Udaipur

A majestic palace complex on the banks of Lake Pichola — the "City of Lakes" at its finest.

Udaipur, Rajasthan
Jaisalmer
Jaisalmer Fort

The "Golden Fort" rising from the Thar Desert — one of the world's largest fully preserved medieval forts.

Jaisalmer, Rajasthan

Uttar Pradesh

Home to the Taj Mahal, Varanasi, and the spiritual heartland of India

Agra
Taj Mahal

UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders — a white marble monument of eternal love.

Agra, Uttar Pradesh
Varanasi
Varanasi Ghats

The spiritual capital of India — ancient ghats along the Ganges where traditions have thrived for millennia.

Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh
Agra
Agra Fort

A massive red sandstone fort that served as the main residence of the Mughal Emperors.

Agra, Uttar Pradesh

Kerala

God's Own Country — backwaters, beaches, hill stations and Ayurveda

Alleppey
Kerala Backwaters

Network of lagoons, lakes and canals — experience Kerala on a traditional houseboat.

Alleppey, Kerala
Munnar
Munnar Tea Gardens

Rolling hills covered with emerald tea plantations — a breathtaking hill station in the Western Ghats.

Munnar, Kerala
Thiruvananthapuram
Kovalam Beach

A crescent-shaped beach with lighthouse, resorts and Ayurvedic treatments along the Arabian Sea.

Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala

Goa

India's beach paradise — golden sands, Portuguese heritage and vibrant nightlife

North Goa
Baga Beach

One of Goa's most popular beaches with water sports, shacks and a lively nightlife scene.

North Goa
Panaji
Basilica of Bom Jesus

UNESCO World Heritage church housing the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier — a Portuguese gem.

Old Goa, Panaji
South Goa
Palolem Beach

A tranquil crescent of golden sand in South Goa — perfect for swimming and kayaking.

South Goa

Maharashtra

From Mumbai's skyline to ancient caves — a state of contrasts and grandeur

Mumbai
Gateway of India

The iconic arch monument overlooking the Arabian Sea — built to commemorate King George V's visit.

Mumbai, Maharashtra
Aurangabad
Ajanta Caves

UNESCO World Heritage rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments with extraordinary ancient paintings and sculptures.

Aurangabad, Maharashtra
Pune
Lonavala Hill Station

A popular hill station near Mumbai and Pune with lush greenery, waterfalls and scenic valleys.

Lonavala, Maharashtra

Tamil Nadu

Ancient Dravidian temples, classical arts and the Coromandel coastline

Madurai
Meenakshi Amman Temple

A stunning 2500-year-old temple with 14 gateway towers covered in thousands of colorful sculptures.

Madurai, Tamil Nadu
Ooty
Ooty Hill Station

The "Queen of Hill Stations" in the Nilgiri Hills — famous for its toy train, tea gardens and botanical gardens.

Ooty, Tamil Nadu
Chennai
Marina Beach

The world's second longest urban beach stretching 13km along the Bay of Bengal.

Chennai, Tamil Nadu

Himachal Pradesh

Snow-capped peaks, apple orchards and adventure in the Himalayas

Manali
Rohtang Pass

A high mountain pass in the Himalayas offering spectacular views and access to Lahaul and Spiti valleys.

Manali, Himachal Pradesh
Shimla
Shimla — Queen of Hills

The charming former summer capital of British India with colonial architecture and scenic mountain walks.

Shimla, Himachal Pradesh
Dharamshala
McLeod Ganj

Home to the Dalai Lama and the Tibetan government-in-exile — a blend of Indian and Tibetan culture.

Dharamshala, Himachal Pradesh

Gujarat

Land of lions, the Rann of Kutch and the birthplace of Gandhi

Kutch
Rann of Kutch

The world's largest salt desert — a surreal white landscape that glows under the full moon sky.

Kutch, Gujarat
Somnath
Somnath Temple

One of the 12 Jyotirlinga shrines of Shiva — a magnificent seaside temple with a glorious history.

Somnath, Gujarat
Junagadh
Gir National Park

The only home of the Asiatic Lion in the world — a must-visit for wildlife enthusiasts.

Junagadh, Gujarat
